05/07/2026 - 15:26

Goodnight Punpun

I've been reading Goodnight Punpun - nothing but praise for it, it's great. I'm not very good at putting my feelings into words, but if anyone likes dark psychological dramas/tragedies then you must 100% read Punpun!!!

Some part of me relates to Punpun, it could be the alienation, shame, dysmorphia. His suffering is so extreme and mine is comparatively mundane, claiming it resonates with me is pretty presumptuous and downright rude, since i've experienced nothing as close to what he has. I'm not saying there's a set "amount" of suffering one must go through before they can feel what Punpun has, because of course, one person's minor inconvenience might be another's pain. This topic is pretty hard to write about in a coherent way. At the end of the day, no one can claim their pain is more legitimate or "real" than someone else's. I've been in so many situations where a person would compare their issues with mine, and tell me that "I haven't been through anything" or I "haven't seen real pain"- something like that. I find it so infuriating, because it seems like people want to prove they're better than you in any way shape or form (even if it's self-destructive).

Dreams

Do you ever have a dream where when you wake up you feel absolutely destroyed that it was dream? It feels like I lost a part of myself, even though it lasted 30 minutes. Those 30 minutes were pure bliss and I believe to be the result of my brain subconsciously processing what I desire the most.

I tried to learn how to lucid dream as a child, but I never really got the hang of it. I always had the wildest imagination and I soo wanted to somehow translate that into reality, which then led to the topic of dreams, and we all know that scenarios in dreams are often easier to recreate than in real life.

I'd like to believe that dreams reveal hidden truths, or predict the future, but I'm not really superstitious.

06/07/2026 - IT HAPPENED AGAIN

I've had enough. I'm going to learn how to lucid dream for real this time. Why does it always happen the literal minute before my alarm is supposed to go off? It's like my brain is doing it out of spite right as I'm supposed to wake up.

Research - Dreams mostly happen during REM sleep, and the best way to maximise it is to sleep 8-9 hours. Problem is, I only sleep around 7. I'm also going to start keeping a dream journal, in order to train my brain to recall dreams better. What I've learnt first hand is that when I go to sleep thinking about something in particular, that thing or a similar subject also appears in my dream.
